7 Discussion

The results show a clear upward trend in the average U.S. viewership of Game of Thrones. The average audience increased from 2.52 million viewers in season 1 to 11.99 million viewers in season 8.

The highest average viewership was recorded in season 8, with 11.99 million viewers. The largest increase occurred between season 6 and season 7, when average viewership rose by 2.57 million viewers.

By contrast, the smallest increase happened between season 4 and season 5, when average viewership increased by only 0.04 million viewers. Overall, the show gained 9.47 million average viewers from the first season to the last season.

These patterns suggest that the series became increasingly popular over time, especially in its later seasons. Although the growth was not equally strong every year, the general trend was clearly positive.

8 Conclusion

This report analyzed the season-level viewership of Game of Thrones. Based on the available data, the show experienced substantial audience growth across its eight seasons. Both the line chart and the season-to-season change chart show that the series attracted a much larger average audience in the final seasons than at the beginning.
