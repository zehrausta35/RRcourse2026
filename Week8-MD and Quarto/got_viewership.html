About the Show

Game of Thrones is an American fantasy drama television series created by David Benioff and D. B. Weiss for HBO. Based on George R. R. Martin’s novel series A Song of Ice and Fire, the show premiered on April 17, 2011, and concluded on May 19, 2019, spanning eight seasons and 73 episodes.

Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, Game of Thrones follows several noble houses competing for the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms, while an ancient threat stirs beyond a massive wall in the north. The series became one of the most-watched programmes in HBO history and won a record 59 Primetime Emmy Awards during its run.

Season-to-Season Changes

Observations

Viewership for Game of Thrones grew almost every season. The largest single jump came between Seasons 6 and 7, when average viewers rose by 2.57 million. The one near-flat transition was between Seasons 4 and 5, where the change was just 0.04 million. By the final season the show had attracted 11.99 million average viewers per episode — 376% more than when it first aired.
