Game of Thrones – TV Series Overview

Game of Thrones is a landmark HBO fantasy drama series based on George R. R. Martin’s epic novel saga A Song of Ice and Fire. Set across the continents of Westeros and Essos, the show follows brutal political conflicts, shifting alliances, and the struggle for dominance over the Iron Throne.

The narrative centers on three major, interwoven story arcs:

  • The War for the Iron Throne — A complex web of political intrigue as the Great Houses (Stark, Lannister, Baratheon, Targaryen, and others) battle for control of the Seven Kingdoms.
  • The Rise of Daenerys Targaryen — Her journey from exile to becoming a powerful contender for the throne, supported by dragons and loyal followers.
  • The Threat Beyond the Wall — The Night King and the White Walkers pose an existential danger to all of humanity, forcing old enemies to unite.

The series is known for its morally ambiguous characters, unexpected plot twists, and cinematic world‑building.

Interpretation of Viewership Changes

Across Season 1 of Game of Thrones, viewership varied noticeably from episode to episode.
The season opened with 2.22 million viewers and ended with
3.04 million,
representing 0.82 million overall change.

The largest single‑episode increase was 0.38 million,
and the largest drop was -0.14 million.

On average, episodes attracted 2.52 million viewers,
and the season shows a slight increase in audience engagement.

These trends are clearly reflected in the episode‑to‑episode bar chart above.
